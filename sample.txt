OpenAI was founded in December 2015 by Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, Elon Musk,
and several other researchers. The company is headquartered in San Francisco, California.

Sam Altman serves as the CEO of OpenAI. He previously led Y Combinator, the renowned startup
accelerator based in Silicon Valley. Greg Brockman is the President and co-founder of OpenAI,
having earlier worked at Stripe as its first CTO.

In 2019, Microsoft made a significant investment of $1 billion in OpenAI, forging a strategic
partnership. This was followed by a further multi-billion dollar investment in 2023. OpenAI
subsequently integrated its GPT models into Microsoft Azure, Microsoft's cloud computing platform.

OpenAI released GPT-4 in March 2023, a large language model that demonstrated remarkable
reasoning capabilities. GPT-4 powers ChatGPT Plus, a subscription product launched by OpenAI.
ChatGPT itself was released in November 2022 and quickly became the fastest-growing consumer
application in history, reaching 100 million users in just two months.

Anthropic, a competing AI safety company, was founded in 2021 by Dario Amodei and Daniela
Amodei, both former OpenAI executives. Anthropic is headquartered in San Francisco and created
the Claude family of AI assistants. Dario Amodei serves as CEO of Anthropic, while Daniela
Amodei is the President.

Google has invested heavily in Anthropic, committing up to $300 million in 2023, and later
expanded its investment as part of a broader cloud partnership. Anthropic uses Google Cloud
infrastructure for training its models.
